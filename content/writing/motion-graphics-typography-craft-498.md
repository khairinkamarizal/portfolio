Motion graphics typography is a discipline at the intersection of static type design and temporal design. The rules of static typography apply — legibility, hierarchy, spacing — but they're complicated by the dimension of time. Type now has duration, velocity, and a relationship to what came before and what comes after.

The designers who do this well understand both domains. The ones who struggle usually understand static type but haven't internalized what time adds to the equation.

## Time Is a Design Element

In static design, the viewer controls how long they spend with each element. In motion design, you control it for them. A word on screen for half a second communicates differently from the same word on screen for two seconds.

Duration communicates: importance, contemplation, weight. Short duration: urgency, pace, excitement. Long duration: significance, gravity, reflection. These aren't absolute associations but tendencies that need to match the content.

I set the duration of every typographic element as a design decision, not as a function of when I needed to cut to the next thing. How long should this word be seen? What does that duration say about this word?

## Reading Speed vs. Feeling Speed

One of the most common motion type mistakes: setting type on screen for exactly as long as it takes an average viewer to read it. The result feels rushed and leaves no emotional resonance.

Type on screen needs to be visible for longer than the reading time, especially for short phrases and single words. The reading is instant. The feeling takes longer to register.

My rough guideline: multiply the estimated reading time by 1.5 to 2.5. A two-word phrase that takes 0.5 seconds to read should typically be on screen for 1 to 1.25 seconds. This allows the viewer to read comfortably and feel the content before the cut.

## Reveal Strategies and Their Meaning

How type enters the frame communicates something about the content. The major reveal strategies and their expressive character:

**Cut on.** Type appears instantaneously. Communicates: directness, confidence, impact. Works for single words or short phrases that need to hit hard.

**Fade on.** Type gradually becomes visible. Communicates: emergence, uncertainty, softness. Works for contemplative content or gentle reveals.

**Letter-by-letter.** Each letter appears in sequence. Communicates: building, anticipation, careful construction. Can feel tense or playful depending on the pace.

**Word-by-word.** Words appear sequentially. Communicates: unfolding thought, spoken language rhythm. Works for statements read as speech.

**Wipe reveal.** Type is revealed behind a moving boundary. Communicates: uncovering, unveiling, revelation. The direction of the wipe adds directional meaning.

Each of these is a deliberate choice that contributes to meaning. Using fade-on as the default because it looks smooth isn't a creative decision; it's a non-decision.

## Kinetic Typography: When Type Moves to Communicate

Kinetic typography is type that moves as part of its meaning. Words that grow to emphasize. Sentences that shake to communicate anxiety. Letters that fall apart to communicate dissolution.

This technique works when the motion is the message, not just decoration. A word that grows to communicate "more" is kinetic typography at its most direct. A word that rotates because rotation looks interesting is motion for its own sake.

The test: if you described the motion verbally, would it add meaning? "The word gets bigger" adds meaning if the concept is expansion or emphasis. "The word rotates 360 degrees" rarely adds meaning.

## Hierarchy in Time

In static design, hierarchy is spatial. In motion design, hierarchy is also temporal. The most important thing appears first, or largest, or longest. Time can substitute for or reinforce spatial hierarchy.

A title card structure: primary element enters, holds, then secondary elements join. The primary element's duration before the secondary elements appear establishes its dominance. The viewer processes the primary completely before additional information is introduced.

This temporal hierarchy is as controllable and as meaningful as spatial hierarchy. Use it deliberately.
