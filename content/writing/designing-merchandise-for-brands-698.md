Brand merchandise is often treated as an afterthought: take the logo, put it on a t-shirt, call it done. The result is merchandise that communicates "we have a logo" rather than "we have a brand." The missed opportunity is significant, because well-designed merchandise is one of the most intimate brand touchpoints available — something worn, carried, or used daily.

## Merchandise as Brand Extension

The best branded merchandise extends the brand's visual language beyond the logo. It uses the brand's colors, the brand's graphic sensibility, the brand's personality — in formats that work for three-dimensional objects and wearables.

A brand with a strong geometric mark might create merchandise using the geometric forms of the mark at large scale as pattern or graphic element, without always including the full logo. A brand with a distinctive typographic voice might produce merchandise that's primarily typographic — a phrase or word set in the brand typeface at a striking scale.

The goal is merchandise that feels unmistakably of the brand without being a walking billboard.

## Format-Specific Design

Different merchandise formats have different design requirements, and the logo-on-everything approach fails to account for them.

**Apparel:** The graphic needs to work on a curved surface (the body), be produced in a durable process (screen print, embroidery, heat transfer), and read correctly at a scale appropriate for a garment. Full-color photography doesn't translate well to garment embroidery. Complex fine-line graphics don't survive wash cycles on screen-printed tees.

**Bags and accessories:** Flat surfaces at various sizes. The graphic area might be much larger than a typical print format, or much more constrained, depending on the item. Both require format-specific design rather than logo placement.

**Stationery and paper goods:** Notebooks, cards, wrapping paper. These surfaces often allow for more elaborate graphic treatment — full-bleed pattern, rich color, detailed illustration — than apparel.

**Hard goods:** Mugs, water bottles, pins, phone cases. Small surfaces that may be irregular or curved. The graphic needs to be simple enough to work at small scale and adapt to the specific surface geometry.

## The Limited Edition Principle

The best branded merchandise often has some quality of being collectible or limited — a specific design for a specific event, a collaboration, an annual edition. This framing changes the perceived value from "branded commodity" to "object worth having."

Even if the production run is large, treating merchandise design as a specific design decision rather than a logo application produces work that feels more considered and is therefore more valued.

## Production Quality Communicates Brand Value

The quality of merchandise production signals brand values as clearly as any other brand touchpoint. A brand that positions itself as premium but produces low-quality merchandise is communicating inconsistently. A brand that positions itself as environmentally conscious but produces fast-fashion promotional goods is communicating inconsistently.

I specify merchandise production with the same attention I give to print production: material quality, print or embroidery quality, packaging if applicable. The merchandise experience from opening the packaging to daily use is all brand communication.
