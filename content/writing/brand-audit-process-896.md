## Before Recommending, Investigate

When a client comes to me and says their brand isn't working, my first instinct is not to start designing. It's to understand what "not working" means specifically, and whether the visual identity is actually the problem.

A brand audit — a systematic review of the existing brand's visual health across all touchpoints — is the tool I use to answer that question. It takes a few hours to do properly and typically prevents much more expensive misdirection later.

## What a Brand Audit Covers

A thorough brand audit examines:

**Consistency**: are the logo, colors, and typography being applied consistently across all touchpoints? Inconsistency is the most common visual brand problem and often the most fixable.

**Currency**: does the visual identity reflect the brand's current positioning and audience? An identity designed for a different brand stage often needs updating, not wholesale replacement.

**Effectiveness**: does the identity communicate what the brand needs to communicate in each context? A logo that works on a website might fail on a vehicle graphic or embroidered uniform.

**Differentiation**: does the identity look distinct from key competitors? Category parity can develop over time as design trends spread across an industry.

**Quality of execution**: regardless of concept, is the mark well-constructed? Is the typography professionally set? Is the color system sound?

## The Touchpoint Inventory

I collect samples of every major brand touchpoint: the website, business cards, signage, social profiles, email templates, printed collateral, vehicle graphics if applicable, staff uniforms. I look at all of them together before evaluating any individual piece.

The full picture almost always reveals something that spot-checking individual pieces doesn't. A brand that looks coherent on the website might look completely different on printed materials produced by a different vendor or an internal team without guidelines.

## The Honest Finding

The most valuable outcome of a brand audit is an honest finding: here is what is working, here is what is broken, and here is the minimum change required to address the problems. Sometimes the finding is "the identity is sound but application is inconsistent — guidelines and templates will solve this." Sometimes it's "the mark is structurally weak and needs redesign." Sometimes it's "the identity is fine but the positioning it's built on is wrong."

Each finding has a different recommended action and a different cost. The audit separates these clearly so the client can make an informed decision about where to invest.
