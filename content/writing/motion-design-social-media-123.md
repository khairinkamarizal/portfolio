## The Social Motion Problem

Motion design for social media is not the same as motion design for broadcast or film. The context is different, the constraints are different, and the viewer behavior is radically different. Applying broadcast motion principles to social content without adaptation produces work that doesn't perform.

Understanding the specific constraints of the platform is where good social motion design starts.

## Loops Are the Native Format

Social video is often consumed as a loop. The viewer doesn't necessarily watch from beginning to end — they land somewhere in the middle, the content loops, they watch until they scroll away. This means a great social motion piece has no clear beginning or end. It loops seamlessly, and any moment in the loop is a valid entry point.

Designing a seamless loop is a specific skill. The end of the animation has to transition back to the beginning without a visible seam. The content within the loop has to be compelling at any random entry point.

## No Audio by Default

Most social media content is consumed without audio. Autoplay mute is the default on most platforms. This means motion design for social has to work completely in silence.

This changes the relationship between motion and meaning. In broadcast motion work, audio — music, sound effects, voiceover — carries a significant portion of the communication. Strip that out and the visual motion has to work harder. Every beat has to be visible, not just audible.

## Short and Punchy

Attention on social platforms is scarce. The window for capturing and holding a viewer is measured in seconds. Social motion pieces tend to be much shorter than broadcast equivalents — often under 15 seconds, sometimes under 6.

This compression forces economy. Every second has to earn its place. There's no room for slow builds or extended transitions. The content has to be engaging immediately and throughout.

## The Square and Vertical Formats

Social platforms have popularized square (1:1) and vertical (9:16) aspect ratios alongside the traditional horizontal (16:9). Motion design for social often needs to work in multiple formats simultaneously.

Designing for aspect ratio flexibility from the start is much easier than adapting horizontal compositions to vertical after the fact. I build social motion systems with the full range of formats in mind before committing to layout decisions.

## Text Legibility Under Compression

Social video is often heavily compressed, which degrades fine detail. Text that reads clearly at source quality can become illegible after platform compression artifacts. I use heavier type weights and larger sizes than would be strictly necessary at native quality, to ensure legibility survives the compression pipeline.

Testing with platform-compressed versions before finalizing is essential. What you see in your compositor is not what your viewers see.
