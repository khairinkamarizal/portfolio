I've described pieces of my logo design process in various articles but haven't laid out the complete sequence in one place. Here it is: the full process from brief receipt to final mark delivery.

## Stage 1: Brief Deep Dive (Day 1-2)

I read the brief thoroughly and then sit with it for a day before doing anything else. I'm asking: do I understand the brand well enough to design for it? What's still unclear?

I write a brief summary in my own words: who this brand is, who it's for, what it needs to communicate, and what would make this identity successful. If I can't write this clearly, I need more information before I start.

I also research the competitive landscape and collect visual reference. The competitive research is about context; the visual reference is about calibration.

## Stage 2: Concept Development (Day 2-4)

I generate concepts on paper, not in software. Sketches, diagrams, written concepts — the goal is to explore a wide range of possible directions quickly, without the quality pressure that software creates.

I'm looking for three to four conceptually distinct territories: different ideas about what this brand could be visually. Not three variations of the same idea, but three genuinely different answers to the brief.

## Stage 3: Direction Selection (Day 4-5)

I evaluate the concepts against the brief and select the two or three strongest directions to develop further. The selection criteria are the questions I described in the "reviewing brand concepts" article: does it answer the brief, is it defensible, does it differentiate, is it flexible?

One direction may be clearly right and two supporting options. Two directions may be roughly equal and require further development to evaluate. Whatever the case, I'm going into development with a small, strong field.

## Stage 4: Initial Development (Day 5-8)

In Illustrator, I develop each selected direction to a presentation-ready state. Not final — but resolved enough to communicate the concept and evaluated fairly.

I work on each concept fully before moving to the next. I don't jump between concepts mid-development; it introduces cross-contamination between directions.

## Stage 5: Presentation Preparation (Day 8-9)

I build the presentation, which means: writing concept statements for each direction, creating context applications (a business card, a digital application, a relevant branded surface), and preparing a presentation structure.

The presentation is designed as a communication, not just a display of work.

## Stage 6: Client Presentation and Feedback (Day 9-10)

I present, the client responds, I listen and take notes. I ask clarifying questions about the feedback. I leave with a clear direction to develop further.

## Stage 7: Refinement (Day 10-14)

One direction receives full development attention: mark refinement, construction geometry, spacing and clear space rules, color palette, secondary configurations.

## Stage 8: System Development (Day 14-18)

The refined mark gets built into a minimal viable logo system: primary and secondary configurations, color variants, file formats. The delivery package is organized.

## Stage 9: Presentation and Delivery

Final presentation, client approval, delivery of files and basic usage documentation.

This timeline is approximate and varies significantly with project scope and client responsiveness. The sequence, however, is consistent.
