## The full arc in six phases

A comprehensive visual identity project has a shape — a series of phases that each build on the last, from strategic discovery to final delivery. Understanding the full arc before starting is what prevents the most common project failure modes: starting design too early, presenting too many directions, delivering without context.

This is how I run a full identity project from the first meeting to the last file delivery.

## Phase one: discovery

Discovery is research. I'm learning about the business — its history, its competitive position, its customers, its aspirations, its current pain points. I interview stakeholders. I study competitors. I audit existing brand materials. I collect reference work that resonates with the team and analyze what they're responding to.

Discovery typically takes one to two weeks and produces a research document that synthesizes what I learned and frames the design problem. This document is the agreed-upon strategic foundation for all the design work that follows. Getting the client to review and approve it before proceeding is essential — it prevents strategic misalignment from emerging as aesthetic disagreement in the review phases.

## Phase two: strategy

From the research, I develop a brand strategy: positioning statement, brand personality description, target audience profile, key differentiators. This is not design work — it's writing. But it's the writing that makes the design work possible.

The strategy phase ends with a presentation and client approval. What we're approving is not what the brand will look like — it's what the brand will say and to whom.

## Phase three: concept development

With strategy approved, I move to design. I generate multiple conceptual directions — typically three — each representing a genuinely different interpretation of the strategy. Each direction includes a primary mark concept, a color direction, and a typography direction. No full systems yet; just enough to communicate the concept clearly.

## Phase four: refinement

One direction is selected and refined. This phase is iterative — typically two to three rounds of refinement, each moving the selected direction toward final quality. The mark gets geometric construction refinement. The color palette gets full specification. The typography system gets built out.

## Phase five: system development

With the primary mark finalized, the full system is developed: all mark variants, complete color specifications, the full typography system, spacing rules, primary applications (stationery, key digital applications). This is where the identity becomes a system rather than a mark.

## Phase six: delivery

Final delivery includes: all mark files in every required format (AI, EPS, SVG, PDF, PNG at multiple resolutions, each variant), complete brand guidelines documentation, application templates for key brand touchpoints, and a handoff conversation with the team who will implement the brand.

The delivery isn't the end — it's the beginning of the brand's life. A well-constructed delivery sets that life up for success.
