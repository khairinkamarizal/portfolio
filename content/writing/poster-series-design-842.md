## A Series Is a System

Designing a single poster is a composition problem. Designing a poster series is a systems problem. The individual pieces need to work as independent compositions and as a coherent family — and these requirements sometimes conflict.

The conflict: variety that makes each poster interesting to look at individually can create visual incoherence across the series. Strict consistency that makes the series cohesive can make individual pieces feel formulaic.

Navigating this tension is the central design challenge of series work.

## The Fixed and Variable Framework

My approach to poster series design uses a fixed and variable framework. Some elements are fixed across all posters — they're the constant that defines the series family. Other elements vary — they're what makes each poster distinct.

The fixed elements are typically: grid system, color palette, primary typeface, overall compositional logic. The variable elements are typically: specific imagery or illustration, secondary color use, specific typographic treatments, compositional energy.

Deciding what to fix and what to vary is the core design decision of a series brief. Fix too much and the series is boring. Fix too little and it's incoherent.

## The Danger of Starting with the Most Interesting Piece

A common mistake in series design is starting with the most exciting, most expressive piece and then trying to match the rest of the series to it. The problem: the constraints that made the exciting piece work don't necessarily transfer cleanly to the rest of the series.

I start with the system first. Define the fixed elements, define the range of variation, then design all pieces together rather than one at a time. This ensures the system is actually generative — capable of producing many different pieces within a consistent framework — rather than retrospectively discovered.

## Typography Across a Series

Typography is usually the strongest cohesion tool in a series. A consistent type system — specific faces, specific weight relationships, consistent hierarchy logic — creates family resemblance even across wildly different compositions.

I'll often allow imagery and color to vary significantly within a series while keeping the typographic treatment nearly identical. The type becomes the signature of the series.

## The Layout Together Test

Before finalizing a poster series, I lay all pieces out together on one page and evaluate them as a group. This test reveals:

- Whether the series reads as a family at a glance
- Whether any piece reads as an outlier that breaks the family feel
- Whether the variation between pieces has the right character — interest without incoherence
- Whether the overall effect of the series is greater than the sum of its parts

A series that passes the layout together test is finished. One that doesn't needs either a system revision or a specific piece revision.
